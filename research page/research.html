Introduction

Research Exposure for Students and Scholars

We offer limited, high-quality research exposure opportunities aligned with our ongoing discovery and experimental programs.

Participants engage with real laboratory workflows across microbial bioprospecting, bioactive discovery, and in-vitro validation systems.

Participants may align their exposure with application domains such as pharmaceutical, agricultural, industrial, environmental, and life sciences, within the framework of real bioprospecting and discovery workflows.

Not Routine Training Modules These are structured research exposure programs designed to provide hands-on understanding of applied biotechnology and discovery-driven workflows.
Selective Participation Participation is selective and aligned with ongoing research activities and laboratory capacity.
Real Laboratory Workflows Engage directly with active discovery programs spanning bioprospecting, metabolite discovery, and in-vitro validation.
Program Tracks

Program Tracks

Explore our structured research exposure tracks, each designed to immerse participants in a specific domain of applied biotechnology and discovery science.

01
Focus

Microbial Bioprospecting & Isolation Track

Exploration, isolation, and comprehensive characterization of microorganisms from diverse environmental samples.

  • Environmental sampling strategies (soil, water, niche habitats)
  • Isolation and purification of microbial strains
  • Morphological and biochemical characterization
  • Molecular identification of microbial strains (16S rRNA for bacteria / ITS for fungi)
  • Basic bioinformatics analysis for sequence identification and phylogenetic insights
  • Culture preservation and maintenance techniques
02
Focus

Molecule & Metabolite Discovery Track

Identification and screening of bioactive metabolites from microbial and plant sources.

  • Microbial metabolite extraction techniques
  • Phytochemical extraction and fractionation
  • Bioactivity-guided screening approaches
  • Preliminary identification of active fractions
03
Focus

Metabolite Production & Bioprocess Track

Optimization of microbial systems for targeted metabolite production.

  • Media formulation and optimization
  • Fermentation parameter tuning (pH, temperature, nutrients, aeration)
  • Strain-specific cultivation strategies
  • Understanding multi-metabolite expression in microbial systems
04
Focus

Bioactivity & In-vitro Validation Track

Functional evaluation of bioactive compounds.

  • Antimicrobial assays
  • Enzyme inhibition studies
  • Cytotoxicity screening methods
  • In-vitro pharmacological models
05
Focus

Cell Culture & Anticancer Screening Track

Cell-based evaluation of bioactive molecules.

  • Cell culture handling techniques
  • Cytotoxicity assays (MTT and related methods)
  • Anticancer screening workflows
  • Experimental design for cell-based studies
Program Structure

Program Structure

Our programs are structured to offer meaningful, immersive research exposure through hands-on laboratory work, guided experimental workflows, and direct integration with ongoing discovery programs.

Opportunities are limited and curated — we only onboard scholars whose work aligns with our active discovery programs.

Short-term Exposure

2–4 weeks of focused, hands-on immersion in a selected research track.

Medium-term Programs

4–8 weeks of deeper engagement with guided experimental workflows and discovery programs.

Project-based Engagement

Structured project-based engagement for M.Sc and PhD scholars aligned with ongoing research.

Concept-to-Application

Integration with ongoing discovery programs for concept-to-application understanding of applied biotechnology.

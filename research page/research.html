Molmet Biotech offers limited research exposure opportunities aligned with ongoing discovery, experimental, and translational biotechnology programs. Participants engage with real laboratory workflows across microbial bioprospecting, bioactive discovery, metabolite investigation, and in-vitro validation systems. These programs are designed for individuals seeking practical understanding of applied biotechnology within active research environments.

Research Immersion Approach

Not Routine Training. Selective Immersion.

These are not routine academic training modules or standardized laboratory courses. Our programs are structured as selective research immersion experiences designed around ongoing scientific activities, biological exploration, and application-focused experimentation.

Participation is aligned with laboratory capacity, research relevance, and scientific engagement objectives.

Structured exposure to discovery, experimentation, and translational research culture.

Research Immersion Tracks

01

Microbial Discovery & Bioprospecting

Exposure to environmental sampling, microbial isolation, molecular identification, and basic bioinformatics workflows associated with microbial discovery programs.

Exposure Includes
  • Environmental sampling strategies
  • Isolation and characterization of microbial strains
  • 16S rRNA / ITS-based molecular identification
  • Sequence analysis and phylogenetic interpretation
  • Culture preservation and maintenance
02

Molecule & Metabolite Discovery

Exposure to workflows associated with extraction, screening, and preliminary evaluation of microbial metabolites and natural bioactive compounds.

Exposure Includes
  • Microbial metabolite extraction techniques
  • Phytochemical extraction and nanoparticle synthesis
  • Bioactivity-oriented screening approaches
  • Fractionation and preliminary evaluation workflows
03

Metabolite Production & Bioprocess Systems

Exposure to fermentation optimization and microbial cultivation strategies associated with targeted metabolite production.

Exposure Includes
  • Media formulation and optimization
  • Fermentation parameter tuning (pH, temperature, nutrients, aeration)
  • Strain-specific cultivation approaches
  • Multi-metabolite expression concepts in microbial systems
04

Bioactivity & In-vitro Validation

Exposure to biological evaluation systems used for functional assessment of compounds, metabolites, and biological systems.

Exposure Includes
  • Antimicrobial and biochemical assays
  • Enzyme inhibition studies
  • Cytotoxicity and functional screening
  • In-vitro pharmacological evaluation models

Program Structure

Programs are designed to provide guided exposure to laboratory environments, scientific workflows, and experimental systems associated with ongoing research activities.

Engagement Formats

  • Short-term immersion (2–4 weeks)
  • Medium-term research exposure (4–8 weeks)
  • Project-linked engagement for M.Sc. and PhD scholars

Program Elements

  • Guided laboratory participation
  • Workflow-oriented learning
  • Exposure to real research systems
  • Integration with ongoing discovery programs
Focused exposure to scientific workflows operating within real discovery environments.

Who Should Apply

Students in Life Sciences

B.Sc, B.Tech, M.Sc. & M.Tech students in Biotechnology, Microbiology, and Life Sciences seeking active research exposure beyond classrooms.

PhD Scholars & Researchers

PhD scholars and early-stage researchers looking to deepen applied research skills within discovery-driven laboratory environments.

Applied Biotech Enthusiasts

Individuals interested in applied biotechnology and discovery-oriented research who seek practical, real-world scientific understanding.
